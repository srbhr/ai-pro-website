Frontier embedding APIs are good at most things. They are also expensive, versioned beyond your control, and bound by rate limits that do not respect your actual workload. For a narrow domain — a product catalogue, a support corpus, a specific codebase — you can usually train something as good or better in a weekend, and it will keep working when the API price changes.

This essay walks through that exercise end to end. We start with a small distilled base model, a few thousand positive pairs mined from your own data, and a contrastive objective. We talk about hard-negative mining properly — not the bag-of-tricks version, but the thing that actually matters once your batch is big enough. And we finish with an honest evaluation: MRR on your retrieval set, drift between dev and prod, and a few failure modes you will almost certainly hit.

## What you will need

A GPU with ~16 GB, a base model around 100M parameters, and a corpus where you can build a believable "these two things should be close" signal. That signal is the entire game. Everything else is training ceremony.

## What this isn't

A benchmark chase. We are not trying to beat `text-embedding-3-large` on MTEB. We are trying to build a model that works on *your* data, that you understand end to end, and that you can redeploy without paging oncall.
