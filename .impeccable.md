# AI Pro — design context

## Users

AI Pro serves a **mixed audience of three overlapping readers**, and design must not compromise any of them:

1. **Senior ML / infrastructure engineers** — staff+ practitioners shipping AI in production. They skim defensively, expect claims to be backed by running code, and punish hand-waving. They read on big screens at 9pm or on a phone on the train. They will abandon anything that smells like content marketing.
2. **Curious software generalists** — strong engineers who want depth without being AI practitioners yet. They need on-ramps without being talked down to.
3. **Researchers / academics** — expect rigour, citations, math. Bored by product framing; allergic to "thought leadership."

The job to be done is the same across all three: **read a long-form piece end-to-end and come out meaningfully wiser**. Not skim, not snackable, not queued-for-later.

## Brand personality

Three words, non-negotiable: **slow · rigorous · unimpressed**.

- **Slow** — the reading pace is deliberate. Long lines are fine. Animations are rare, and when they happen they're short and have meaning (reading progress, TOC scroll-spy). Nothing that rewards skimming.
- **Rigorous** — details are correct. Typography respects opsz axes. Colours are perceptually-uniform OKLCH. Code examples run. Claims are cited. Nothing ornamental.
- **Unimpressed** — the publication is immune to hype. No hero gradient fireworks, no "AI-powered" copy, no emoji, no sparkles, no "engage more"-style nudges. Quietly confident rather than loud.

Emotional goal: **a reader finishes an essay and feels like the writer respected their time**.

## Aesthetic direction

Editorial-technical — closer to a printed technical manual or a New York Review of Books essay than to a SaaS landing page. Fraunces + Geist pairing is on the right track (italic accents, opsz display typography, OKLCH tokens, chartreuse accent), but **Fraunces itself is being swapped** (see Open design decisions).

**Dark theme is the default.** This is correct: the primary reading context is engineers on big monitors at night and phones late at night.

### Anti-references — flag drift toward any of these

- **Generic SaaS landing** — Vercel / Linear / Resend school. Cards, pricing tiers, "trusted by" logos, feature lists, FAQ, testimonials, big CTA stack.
- **Tech-blog template** — Medium / Dev.to / Hashnode. Sidebars, clap buttons, reading-time badges, "related posts" rail, author bio blocks, "follow" CTAs.
- **AI-blog template** — purple-to-blue gradients, neon accents on dark, "AI-powered" copy, sparkle/star icons, glowing orbs, crystal-ball imagery, synthwave.
- **Newsletter-popup / Substack** — email capture modal, "subscribe for more," post-paywall teases, "from the author" boxes, scroll-triggered popups.

Claudia's designs already avoid these; the job is to not drift into them as we port.

## Design principles

Derived from the above — these override stylistic preference in any disagreement:

1. **Copy before ornament.** If a decorative element doesn't serve reading, cut it. No gradients on text, no decorative sparklines, no purely-visual cards.
2. **Typography is the voice.** Display + body + mono pairing is the strongest identity signal. The italic-accent pattern within headlines is the signature move.
3. **The accent is rare.** 60/30/10 applies to visual weight, not pixel count. Chartreuse should feel earned when it appears.
4. **Reading is the feature.** Everything else — nav, rail, footer, CTAs — is subordinate. Never compete with the article body for attention.
5. **Reject reflex moves.** The skill's reflex-font and visual-ban list (gradient text, side-stripe borders, glass cards, generic card grids, "hero metric layout") describe the generic AI aesthetic we must stay outside of.

## Accessibility

Assumed requirements until told otherwise:

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum for contrast (OKLCH tokens are tuned for this; verify after theme work).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored — reading-progress bar and scroll-spy still work, marquee stops, pulse dot stops.
- Keyboard nav: TOC links, nav, theme toggle, all buttons reachable and operable.
- Sticky header + right rail don't trap keyboard focus.
- Reading body ≥ 16px, line-length ≤ 75ch (our 680px post-body column hits ~65-72ch at current size).

## Open design decisions

- **Serif family swap.** Fraunces is on the impeccable skill's reflex-reject list. User has opted to **swap to a less common variable serif**. Shortlist to propose: Source Serif 4 (free, Adobe, serious variable), Besley (free, Google, slab-adjacent, underused), Tiempos (Klim, paid, the editorial long-form benchmark), Signifier (Klim, paid, rational with personality). Primary recommendation pending review.
- **Accent hue.** Chartreuse #D6FF3D is Claudia's choice. For "slow · rigorous · unimpressed" the hue may be *too loud* — worth evaluating a more restrained accent (muted amber, ink-blue, or a desaturated green) in the status review.
- **Shader system.** Currently a positioned empty placeholder; user will plug in a different shader solution. Placement and opacity contracts are preserved so it drops in cleanly.
